# LinkedIn Prospecting Playbook — Customs Broker Outreach

**Rewind Tariffs | Updated March 2026**

---

## The Opportunity

The Supreme Court's 6-3 ruling in *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump* (Feb 20, 2026) struck down all IEEPA tariffs. Over $175 billion in duties collected between Feb 2025 and Feb 2026 are potentially refundable to 301,000+ importers across 34 million+ entries.

Customs brokers are the natural gatekeepers. They filed the original entries, they hold the documentation, and their clients trust them. A broker who proactively surfaces refund opportunities becomes a hero to their clients. That's our angle.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile

### Tier 1 — High Priority
Licensed customs brokers at firms with 50+ employees, handling 500+ entries/month, located near major port cities. These firms have the highest density of affected importer clients and the operational capacity to process referrals at scale.

**Target titles:** Licensed Customs Broker, VP of Brokerage Operations, Director of Customs, Trade Compliance Manager, Principal/Owner

**Target companies:** Mid-to-large brokerages and freight forwarders with customs brokerage arms. Think C.H. Robinson, Livingston International, A.N. Deringer, Shapiro, Vandegrift, Alba Wheels Up, Customs One, Pacific Customs Brokers, BDP International, and their regional equivalents.

### Tier 2 — Medium Priority
Independent customs brokers (1-50 employees), trade compliance consultants, and freight forwarder executives whose firms also do brokerage. Smaller volume per partner but often faster to close because the decision-maker is the person you're messaging.

### Tier 3 — Opportunistic
Trade association leaders (NCBFAA, local customs broker associations), trade attorneys, and import managers at large importers. These aren't referral partners per se, but they're connectors who can introduce you to Tier 1 and Tier 2 prospects.

## Sales Navigator Search Playbook

### Daily Routine (45-60 minutes)

**Block 1: New Prospect Search (20 min)**
Run one of the four searches below. Rotate daily.

**Block 2: Profile Review & Outreach (25 min)**
Open top prospects, personalize messages, send with Claude's help (always review before sending).

**Block 3: Follow-Up & Engagement (15 min)**
Check for accepted connections, send follow-ups, engage with prospects' posts.

### Search 1: Licensed Customs Brokers
Open Sales Navigator → Lead Search. Title: "customs broker" OR "licensed customs broker" OR "LCB" OR "CHB". Industry: Transportation & Logistics. Geography: United States. Company headcount: 11+. Sort by recently active. Goal: Cast the widest net across the profession.

### Search 2: Brokerage Operations Leaders
Title: "VP brokerage" OR "director customs" OR "head of customs" OR "brokerage manager". Same industry and geography filters. Goal: Target decision-makers who can greenlight a referral partnership.

### Search 3: Named Firm Deep Dives
Pick a specific brokerage firm. Search all employees with relevant titles. Goal: Multiple touchpoints within a single organization — one connection can unlock introductions to the whole firm.

### Search 4: Independent Broker Principals
Title: "owner" OR "president" OR "founder". Industry: International Trade. Company headcount: 2-50. Goal: Reach the person who IS the decision-maker.

## The Outreach Sequence

### Day 0: Connection Request
Send a personalized connection request using templates A1-A6 (see message templates reference). Choose the template that best fits the prospect's profile. Always personalize.

### Day 2-3: If Accepted → Follow-Up 1 (F1)
Thank them and plant the seed. Keep it conversational. Mention you can be a resource if their clients have questions about IEEPA refunds.

### Day 5-7: Follow-Up 2 (F2)
Offer something valuable — the refund paths guide, the eligibility assessment tool, a relevant CIT case update. Lead with value, not a sales pitch.

### Day 10-14: Follow-Up 3 (F3)
If they've engaged at all (liked a post, replied briefly), move to the partnership proposal. Lay out the referral and white-label options. Propose a 15-minute call.

### Day 7 (if no response to F1): Nudge (F4)
A brief, friendly bump. No pressure. Some people are busy — timing matters.

### If Not Accepted After 7 Days
Try an InMail (I1 or I2) if you have credits. Or engage with their content first (comment on a post, react to an article they shared) and then try connecting again in 2-3 weeks.

## Messaging Angles (Match to Prospect)

**For brokers at large firms** → Angle: white-label partnership. "We work behind the scenes. Your firm gets credit for offering the service." These firms care about maintaining the client relationship and adding service lines.

**For independent broker owners** → Angle: referral fees + client retention. "Earn money for introductions and give your clients a reason to stay." Independents are entrepreneurial and respond to revenue opportunities.

**For trade compliance managers** → Angle: operational simplicity. "We handle the assessment and recovery process so your team doesn't have to build it from scratch." Compliance people care about getting it right without adding headcount.

**For brokers who post about tariffs** → Angle: thought leadership + collaboration. "Saw your post about X — would love to compare notes. We're building tools on the refund side." These are your warmest prospects because they're already publicly engaged with the topic.

## What Success Looks Like

**Week 1-2:** Building the pipeline. 150+ connection requests out. Learning what messaging resonates. Refining ICP based on who responds.

**Week 3-4:** First wave of acceptances coming in. Follow-up sequence running. First 2-3 meetings with interested brokers. Starting to see which message variants perform best.

**Month 2:** 5-10 active partnership conversations. First referral agreements signed. Pipeline of 300+ qualified broker contacts. Clear data on best-performing search strategies and message variants.

**Month 3+:** Referral network generating inbound leads. Expanding to Tier 2 and Tier 3 targets. Scaling what works, dropping what doesn't.
